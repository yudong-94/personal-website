### Reading List in Past Two Months  

Now, let's talk about the great articles I came across in the past two months.

#### Data Science & Analytics
1. [**Beyond Code Generation: AI for the Full Data Science Workflow**](https://towardsdatascience.com/beyond-code-generation-ai-for-the-full-data-science-workflow/): AI can now do much more than writing code and docs -- it can automate the full data science workflow. In this article, I shared how I used Codex + MCP to connect Google Drive, GitHub, and BigQuery, and go from raw Apple Health data to a stakeholder-facing report in about 30 minutes.  
2. [**Beyond Prompting: Using Agent Skills in Data Science**](https://towardsdatascience.com/beyond-prompting-using-agent-skills-in-data-science/): I have been making one visualization every week since 2018. In this, I shared how that long-running habit became a practical use case for agent skills in data science and how to use skill in real work environment.  
3. [**Does Causal Inference Overly Rely on Assumptions?**](https://levelup.gitconnected.com/does-causal-inference-overly-rely-on-assumptions-b042775d08f0): Every causal estimate rests on assumptions -- ignorability (no unmeasured confounders), overlap (treatment and control groups actually comparable), and correct model specification (no hidden functional form mistakes).  
4. [**MMM Isn’t Causal: This is How Meta and Google Fix It**](https://pub.towardsai.net/mmm-isnt-causal-this-is-how-meta-and-google-fix-it-47bd0ac0ac94): The central question that MMM is trying to answer is "What would happen to sales if we changed spend?", which is a causal problem. Meta and Google use experimentation to establish causality.  
5. [**Causal Inference Is Different in Business**](https://towardsdatascience.com/causal-inference-is-different-in-business/): Focus on the big business question, find easier alternative solutions if possible, and do 80/20 depends on what it takes to the decision.  


#### Data Career  
1. [**From Dashboards to Decisions: Rethinking Data & Analytics in the Age of AI **](https://towardsdatascience.com/from-dashboards-to-decisions-rethinking-data-analytics-in-the-age-of-ai/): Decision Intelligence that can guide decisions is the next evolution, beyond traditional dashboards.  
2. [**AI Is Coming for Data Science Jobs — But Not in the Way You Think**](https://medium.com/write-a-catalyst/ai-is-coming-for-data-science-jobs-but-not-in-the-way-you-think-712201025236): AI is automating the janitor work. Which means data scientists are finally being redirected toward work requiring creativity, strategic thinking, ethical judgment, and interpersonal skills — the stuff humans are actually good at.  
3. [**The Ultimate Guide to Future-Proofing Your Data Science Career (2026–2027)**](https://medium.com/data-science-collective/the-ultimate-guide-to-future-proofing-your-data-science-career-2026-2027-5b968511e687): How to stay competitive as a data scientist by being AI-powered, learn ML & ML Ops, and becoming a human-first strategist.  
4. [**How to Run 1-on-1s That Your Team Actually Looks Forward To**](https://medium.com/@secur3-et/how-to-run-1-on-1s-that-your-team-actually-looks-forward-to-8c2c0215b411): A manager's role in 1-on-1 should be to listen, ask questions, and find ways to help.  
5. [**Good Data, Bad Decisions: The Analytics Paradox Nobody Talks About**](https://medium.com/data-science-collective/good-data-bad-decisions-the-analytics-paradox-nobody-talks-about-c844421c2633): The common reasons of why a clean pipeline doesn’t produce better decisions.  
6. [**Future of Data Analytics: What’s Next After AI (2026 and Beyond)**](https://medium.com/gitconnected/future-of-data-analytics-whats-next-after-ai-2026-and-beyond-831650bb9ad1): Why data analytics still matters and major shifts with AI.
