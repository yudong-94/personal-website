U.S. parents, 2025

The parent labor-force gap is widest with young children

Fathers' participation stayed above 92% across parent groups in 2025. Mothers' participation was lowest among those with children under 6, widening the gap to 27.3 percentage points.

Note: Rates are annual averages and measure the share working or looking for work. The overall under-18 group overlaps with the two child-age groups. 2025 estimates are 11-month averages excluding October because data were not collected during the federal government shutdown. Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, The Economics Daily.
